Overview

OddEven is an innovative indie game that reinvents cricket by integrating hand signs to play strokes, reminiscent of stone-paper-scissors dynamics. Players engage in thrilling matches where strategic choices and quick thinking determine success on the pitch. Accumulate runs while avoiding getting out, making every decision pivotal for victory.

Additional Details

Originally conceived as a school final project, OddEven served as a platform to learn basic concepts of OOP, data structures, and C++ fundamentals. Initially text-based, the game evolved post-school as I invested free time to integrate graphics using conio library functions. Fond memories fuel my passion for the concept, driving a desire to develop an updated version with enhanced graphics and a multiplayer mode, envisioning reunions with childhood friends for nostalgic gameplay experiences.

Key Features

  • Hand Sign Mechanics: The core gameplay revolves around using hand signs to execute various cricket strokes. Players must anticipate their opponent's move while strategically selecting their own strokes to score runs and avoid getting out.
  • Dynamic Strokes: Each hand sign represents a specific cricket stroke, such as two finges up results two runs, three for three runs till five runs and a single thumbs up results in six runs. The diverse range of strokes adds depth to the gameplay, allowing players to adapt their strategies based on the game situation.
  • Inning Change Mechanism: If both players play the same stroke, it results in the batting side getting out, leading to an inning change. This dynamic element adds tension and excitement to the gameplay, as players must carefully consider their choices to avoid falling victim to predictable patterns.
